# ClimaPod: A Portable Humidity Chamber

A compact, low-cost, portable humidity control chamber designed for small-scale environments and educational or research applications. ClimaPod maintains adjustable and stable humidity levels using controlled air mixing, allowing users to create customizable humidity-controlled environments without the cost or scale of industrial systems.

---

## Overview

ClimaPod was developed as a final project for CBE 3300B by Rithika Ramkumar and Masha Zotova. The device combines chemical engineering principles, embedded systems, and feedback control to regulate relative humidity (RH) inside a portable chamber.

The system uses:
- A humid air stream generated by bubbling air through water
- A dry air stream generated using desiccant beads
- A humidity sensor for real-time RH measurements
- A hybrid control algorithm to regulate pump power and maintain user-defined humidity setpoints

Applications include:
- Plant growth experiments
- Controlled storage environments
- Material testing
- Educational demonstrations
- Small-scale laboratory testing

# Device Operation and Chemical Engineering Principles

## How ClimaPod Works

ClimaPod regulates humidity using two independent air streams. Air is bubbled through water, where evaporation transfers water vapor into the airflow until near saturation. Air passes through desiccant beads, removing moisture before entering the chamber. Both streams enter the chamber and mix together to achieve the desired relative humidity. A humidity sensor continuously measures RH inside the chamber. The controller compares the measured humidity against the user-defined setpoint and adjusts pump power accordingly. The pumps are controlled using PWM (pulse-width modulation), allowing dynamic humidity regulation.

---

## Chemical Engineering Principles

### Convective Transport

Pressure gradients generated by the air pumps drive airflow into the chamber.

### Diffusive Transport

Water vapor diffuses from the liquid phase into the air bubbles during humidification.

### Adsorption

Desiccant beads contain hydrophilic pores that adsorb water molecules from the air stream, reducing humidity.

---

## Relative Humidity Equation

Relative humidity is defined as:

\[
RH = \frac{P_{H_2O}}{P^{sat}_{H_2O}(T)}
\]

where:
- \(P_{H_2O}\) is the partial pressure of water vapor
- \(P^{sat}_{H_2O}(T)\) is the saturation vapor pressure at temperature \(T\)

---

# Control Strategy

## Hybrid Control Algorithm

The system uses a combination of bang-bang control and proportional control. When the humidity error is large, the pumps operate at full power to rapidly approach the setpoint. Once the error falls below approximately 4% RH, pump duty cycles become proportional to the error magnitude.

This hybrid strategy was selected because:
- It is computationally simple
- It avoids importing advanced control libraries
- It minimizes processor load
- It still provides stable humidity regulation

# Current Device Constraints

Current limitations include:

## High Humidity Limitations

At very high humidity levels:
- Water droplets become aerosolized
- Moisture can enter tubing lines

## Low Humidity Overshoot

Small humid-air duty cycles can produce relatively large RH jumps.

## Setpoint Precision

The sliding potentiometer:
- Does not directly map to exact RH values
- Makes highly precise adjustment difficult

## Pump Capacity

Larger chambers would require:
- Stronger pumps
- Higher airflow rates
- Improved chamber circulation
